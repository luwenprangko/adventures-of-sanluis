Our Story

History of San Luis

San Luis, Batangas, originally part of the municipality of Taal, was established as an independent town in 1861, named after Saint Louis of France. The area was inhabited by indigenous groups before the arrival of Spanish colonizers in the 16th century, who introduced Catholicism and new agricultural practices.

During the 19th century, San Luis became known for its agricultural production, particularly rice and coconuts, contributing to the local economy. The town played a role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule, with residents participating in the struggle for independence.

Following the American occupation in the early 20th century, San Luis experienced infrastructure development and modernization. After World War II, the town underwent reconstruction and continued to grow, both in population and economic activity.

Today, San Luis is recognized for its rich cultural heritage, agricultural products, and emerging tourism sector, showcasing its historical significance and natural beauty.
